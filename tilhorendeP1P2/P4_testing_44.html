Administrative Details:

Our client is Heidi Dalåmo. She is a dentist and currently she receives appointment bookings via Valentinlystsenteret's website which is something she would like to change as she works private.

Testing Document:

We wanted to focus on live testing/ “real observation” rather than using google forms. Therefor making the structure of the deliverables of this document a bit different than what may was intended. we tried to get testers form different age and social groups to test our website, since the intended audience for this website is everyone that needs dental care (over 21 years)

We wanted to first watch the test subjects and see how they would interact with the website, and later ask some questions to get more information.
These are the questions we asked:

  • What was your first impression of the website?
  • How do you like the design?
  • Was it easy to navigate around the website?
  • Was there anything that was difficult to understand/ non-intuitive?
  • Do you have other feedback?

Tester Audience:

test person 1, 2, 3:
age: 21-24
job/ study program: student

At first, the user began to navigate around the website on their own, where we observed how the user moved through the pages and between them. We assessed whether there were any aspects that were not as intuitive as intended or if the site was clear and well-organized.

  • The user's initial impression was of a very organized and informative homepage, where the necessary information was easily accessible.
  • The user found it to have a simple design that could have been further developed to create a more professional website.
  • When asked, the user mentioned that it was easy to navigate, and everything felt very intuitive. There was nothing specific that was difficult to understand. Through observation, it was noted that upon the initial impression, the user did not realize that the icons in the footer were direct links to email, phone, etc.
  • The technical aspects of the site were very good, providing a clear and understandable interface where one can easily find necessary information and book appointments. There were quite a few buttons for booking, almost on the verge of being too many, but overall acceptable.

Feedback Summary:

Our testers were mostly very impressed with our website and found the functionality and design good.
However, a few things were mentioned more often than others:

  • There should be a mobile version available
  • The dropdowns could be difficult to understand
  • There could be suggestions in the booking input fields

Your Response:

We tried to take all response, both positive and negative into consideration. Sometimes it was easy to respond with an explanation to why we had choosen to do something in a certain way, instead of only listening to the feedback. But after becoming aware of this, it was easier to just accept the feedback.

To address feedback we would start to look at the most critical feedback comments i.e. problems that prevents users form getting all the information they need form the webpage, problems with navigating to the pages they need ect. Secondly, we would look at the feedback regarding non- critical problems, like efficiency eg. auto-fill in the booking page ect.

The feedback we recieved was mostly things we were already aware of, such as a missing mobile version, but we had decided to not focus on those things. We did not specifically change any of the functionality after testing (except fixing some bugs), but if we were to keep working on our page, this feedback would be a great list to follow to improve the site.
